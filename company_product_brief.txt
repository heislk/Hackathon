CRYPTOSECURE
Detailed Product, Safety, Process, and Team Q&A Brief

Purpose of this document:
This document is meant to prepare a 5-person team to confidently explain what CryptoSecure is, what the product does, how it works, why it matters, how it is safe, and how to answer likely judge questions from product, cybersecurity, AI, and business angles.


1. ONE-SENTENCE COMPANY DESCRIPTION

CryptoSecure is a scam and fraud detection platform that helps individuals and crypto platforms quickly evaluate suspicious wallet addresses, transaction hashes, URLs, and phishing emails, then returns clear risk verdicts and deeper intelligence so users can make safer decisions faster.


2. WHAT THE COMPANY DOES

CryptoSecure helps people answer a very practical question:
"Is this crypto-related thing dangerous, suspicious, or safe enough to trust?"

The company is focused on reducing preventable loss in crypto by turning fragmented and confusing signals into a decision-support experience that is understandable, fast, and actionable.

In plain English:
- A user pastes a wallet address, transaction hash, or suspicious URL into the product.
- A user can also upload a suspicious .eml email file.
- CryptoSecure analyzes the input using chain intelligence, phishing checks, and risk signals.
- The product returns a verdict such as Likely Safe, Needs Review, Suspicious, or High Risk.
- Logged-in users get account-level features such as usage tracking, scan history, and subscription-based access to deeper results.

The company is not just a dashboard and not just an AI wrapper.
It is a decision-support product designed around real scam prevention behavior:
- detect suspicious inputs quickly
- present useful context
- help users slow down before making a costly mistake
- scale from individual use to exchange and enterprise workflows


3. THE CORE PROBLEM CRYPTOSECURE SOLVES

Crypto fraud is hard to navigate because the signals are fragmented.
Users and teams often have:
- a suspicious wallet address
- a transaction hash they do not recognize
- a phishing email
- a fake support message
- a malicious-looking URL
- not enough time to investigate deeply

The current problem is not only lack of data.
The bigger problem is lack of clarity.

People often have access to pieces of information, but not a fast, trustworthy interpretation layer.
That creates several real risks:
- retail users panic and click malicious links
- users send money to malicious wallets
- fraud or support teams spend too much time manually reviewing suspicious cases
- exchanges face user trust issues, support burden, and delayed fraud response

CryptoSecure is built to close that gap between raw signal and safe decision.

6. WHAT HAPPENS WHEN SOMEONE USES THE PRODUCT

The best way to explain the product is as a step-by-step workflow.

Step 1: User submits an input
The user provides one of the following:
- wallet address
- transaction hash
- suspicious URL or domain
- phishing email as a .eml file

Step 2: Input classification
The product determines what kind of input it received.
Examples:
- Ethereum-style wallet address
- transaction hash
- Bitcoin address
- URL
- email file

This classification matters because different kinds of evidence need different review logic.

Step 3: Analysis path selection
Once the input type is identified, the product routes it through the appropriate intelligence path.

For chain-related inputs:
- query chain intelligence providers and local intelligence logic
- determine whether the wallet or transaction appears malicious or suspicious
- examine labels, recent activity, balances, flags, and known malicious matches if available

For URLs:
- run phishing-oriented checks
- estimate whether the URL appears malicious or likely safe

For .eml emails:
- parse the email content locally in-browser
- extract links, domains, crypto addresses, attachments, sender information, and urgency indicators
- score risk signals and summarize why the email appears suspicious or safer

Step 4: Verdict generation
CryptoSecure converts the technical analysis into a plain-language output:
- Likely Safe
- Needs Review
- Suspicious
- High Risk

This matters because users do not want raw output only.
They want a decision they can understand.

Step 5: Result presentation
The user sees:
- the verdict
- relevant signals
- data returned from the analysis path
- richer detail depending on subscription tier

Step 6: Account actions for logged-in users
If the user is authenticated:
- the scan can be saved to scan history
- usage counters are updated
- the user can revisit scans later from the account page


7. HOW THE PRODUCT WORKS TECHNICALLY

This section is useful when judges ask, "What is actually happening under the hood?"

Frontend:
- Built with Vite + React
- The product has pages for:
  - home
  - risk scan
  - login
  - registration
  - account
  - business / enterprise messaging

Backend layer:
- A Vite server plugin acts as the local API layer for auth and usage
- SQLite stores:
  - users
  - sessions
  - scan history
  - email check records

Authentication:
- Users register with name, email, password
- Passwords are hashed using bcrypt
- Sessions are token-based
- Tokens are stored client-side and validated server-side

Data model:
- users table stores identity and subscription tier
- sessions table stores active login sessions
- scan_history stores prior scan results
- email_checks tracks email-analysis usage

Chain intelligence flow:
- Inputs are classified as address, tx hash, or URL
- Chain-related inputs use the chain-intelligence stack
- The product can enrich with provider data and local malicious dataset matching
- It then maps technical signals into human-readable verdicts

Email intelligence flow:
- .eml files are processed locally in the browser
- The app extracts:
  - sender and reply-to values
  - URLs
  - domains
  - crypto addresses
  - attachment names
  - urgency patterns
  - authentication-like indicators
- It scores the message and returns a phishing-oriented result summary

Subscription logic:
- AuthContext manages logged-in user state
- SubscriptionContext determines:
  - current tier
  - usage remaining
  - whether a scan is allowed
  - whether a feature is gated

Gating logic:
- Free users can still use the product, but premium sections are blurred
- This preserves usefulness while clearly showing upgrade value


8. HOW THE PRODUCT STAYS SAFE

This is one of the most important sections for judge questions.

CryptoSecure is designed to be safe in several different ways:

A. Safe for user credentials and identity
- Passwords are not stored in plain text
- Passwords are bcrypt-hashed before storage
- Session tokens are generated server-side
- Sessions can be invalidated on logout

B. Safe in how it handles risky content
- The product is designed to inspect suspicious inputs, not execute them
- URLs are analyzed as inputs, not opened automatically by the user flow
- Email files are parsed for indicators and signals rather than treated as trusted content
- This reduces the chance of users interacting directly with potentially malicious material

C. Safe in user decision-making
- The product is built to slow risky decisions down
- The interface turns panic-driven evidence into structured review
- This is important because scam prevention is partly a UX problem, not only a detection problem

D. Safe in data visibility
- Free-tier users see useful verdicts but not full sensitive detail
- This supports product safety and business logic at the same time
- More advanced detail is intentionally gated behind higher trust / paid tiers

E. Safe in product architecture
- Auth, sessions, usage tracking, and history are handled in a controlled local backend layer
- Scan histories are associated to authenticated users
- The system is structured so product state and risk-review history do not float around loosely in the frontend only

F. Safe in messaging
- The product does not promise impossible certainty
- Verdicts such as Needs Review and Suspicious acknowledge uncertainty honestly
- This is important because overclaiming in security products is dangerous


9. WHY THE PRODUCT IS TRUSTWORTHY

Trustworthiness comes from four things:

1. Clear scope
CryptoSecure focuses on a specific problem:
helping users review suspicious crypto-related evidence quickly and clearly.

2. Structured outputs
Instead of dumping raw intelligence, the product organizes it into verdicts, flags, labels, activity, and summaries.

3. Honest uncertainty
The product allows for "Needs Review" rather than pretending every case is fully deterministic.

4. Safety-first UX
The product is designed to keep users from acting blindly on scam signals.

23. RAPID-FIRE JUDGE QUESTIONS AND SHORT ANSWERS

Q: What problem are you solving?
A: We help users and crypto platforms quickly evaluate suspicious wallets, transactions, URLs, and phishing emails so they can avoid preventable loss.

Q: What is the product?
A: A scam and fraud review platform with risk scanning, verdicts, account history, and subscription-based depth of intelligence.

Q: How does it work?
A: Users submit suspicious evidence, we classify the input, run the appropriate analysis path, and return a verdict plus supporting signals.

Q: Is it secure?
A: Yes. Passwords are bcrypt-hashed, sessions are token-based, user history is tied to authenticated accounts, and suspicious evidence is analyzed rather than trusted.

Q: What is actually built?
A: Scan flow, auth, account page, SQLite-backed history, usage tracking, subscription gating, and result rendering are all implemented.

Q: Why would people use this?
A: Because the hardest part in crypto scams is not finding data, it is knowing what to do with it quickly and safely.

Q: Who is the customer?
A: Individual crypto users first, then advanced users, then exchanges and fraud/security teams.

Q: Why would an exchange pay?
A: To reduce review time, support burden, and trust damage around fraud and phishing incidents.

Q: Why the freemium model?
A: It matches the natural user journey from occasional checks to deeper and higher-volume investigation.

Q: What is your unfair advantage?
A: We are combining multiple evidence types into one clean risk-decision experience instead of treating chain, phishing, and account history as separate disconnected tools.
